About Me

From building Meccano race cars as a kid to diagnosing and repairing my own car today, I've always been fascinated by how things work and the satisfaction of fixing them. This hands-on curiosity naturally led me to engineering, where I could combine my love for problem-solving with practical design and innovation.

I graduated in 2025 with a Bachelor of Engineering Science (BESc) in Mechatronic Systems from Western University, where I gained a strong foundation in robotics, control, image processing and analysis, embedded systems, and other core engineering disciplines. My hands-on experiences include co-op positions in quality and manufacturing engineering, contributing to the Western Engineering robotics teams, and helping design an autonomous tree-planting robot for reforestation. These opportunities allowed me to design, build, and test mechanical and electrical systems, with each project fueling my passion for creating solutions that make a difference.

Beyond engineering, I enjoy golf, skiing, and have had a life-long passion for cars. I'm always looking for new challenges that let me build, tinker, and innovate.

I'm excited to apply my skills and curiosity to new engineering projects and continue building systems that matter.
